# Motivation
My motivation is to learn as much as I can and contribute to the community in any way I can. I want to improve my personal skill and foster an environment where I can keep building and growing.

## Who I am
I am Shemar Marks, a Computer Science final year at The University of the West Indies. I have loved programming for as long as I can remember, however I wasn't capable of being ahead of the curve due to a lack of feasible equipment while I was growing up. Since highschool though, I've tried to absorb as much as I can within this space and learn as much as I can. I do have a few projects that I've been working on that I want to see to completion though.

## Why Mesa
I have not yet used Mesa myself, but after looking through the organizations registered within Google's Summer of Code, my interest was piqued and I would love to contribute to this project in any way I can.

## What I want to learn

I want to learn how Mesa models are structured end to end, from defining agents and environments to running experiments and interpreting results. I’m especially interested in best practices for writing clean, scalable simulations, testing model behavior, and visualizing outcomes in a way that is useful to others. 

## Where I want to go
My goal with contributing to Mesa is to grow from a new contributor into someone who can reliably support the project. In the short term, I see myself helping with beginner-friendly issues, improving documentation, adding tests, and fixing small bugs so I can learn the codebase and development workflow. As I gain experience, I would like to contribute to model features, improve usability for new users, and eventually mentor other first-time contributors. Long term, I want my work to make Mesa more approachable, stable, and useful for the broader community.
